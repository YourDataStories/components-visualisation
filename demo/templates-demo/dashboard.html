Official Development Assistance (ODA)

This dashboard allows you to explore data related to official development assistance (ODA) provided by donor countries (such as the Netherlands) to developing countries all over the world (such as Zimbabwe).

Why use YourDataStories?

YourDataStories aggregates data from various separate datasets into a common “semantic graph” in RDF. In plain English: YourDataStories connects information from different sources in one place. It enables users to access such data easily and to discover relations between data. In such a way, users can browse, search, visualize, and make sense of the aggregated data, rather than chase the original, discrete datasets which may only be accessible to experts.

YourDataStories: Modelling ODA data

YourDataStories looks at ODA broken down into individual “Aid Activities”. An Aid Activity has

  • one or more benefactor and beneficiary countries
  • a duration or date range
  • organisations involved in its implementation
  • a thematic sector (e.g., humanitarian assistance, disaster relief, water supply), and
  • a set of financial transactions (such as “Disbursement” a.k.a. actual payment, or “Commitment” a.k.a. planned payment).

Selected filters

Finally, there is a set of “info boxes” that allow you to filter for various aspects of the ODA data. Clicking on each “info box” shows predefined visualisations. Using the icons in the upper right corner of the visualisation, you can toggle between various types of infographics. The icons in the upper left corner allow you to generate an embed code to use the visualisation on another website, and to add it to your personal data collection (“basket”).
